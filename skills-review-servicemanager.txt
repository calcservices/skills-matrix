SKILLS MATRIX — SERVICE MANAGERS
==================================
Levels: Foundation → Proficient → Advanced → Expert
Format: each level shows what it looks like, followed by how to progress to the next.

CATEGORY: Client & Relationship Management
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Client Relationships
│ Building and maintaining effective relationships with pension scheme clients —
│ understanding their needs, managing expectations, and acting as a trusted
│ point of contact.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Communicates with clients on straightforward matters with guidance.
    Understands who the key client contacts are.
  → To reach Proficient: Shadow a senior manager on client calls. Note how they manage tone, pace, and expectation-setting.

  Proficient
    Manages day-to-day client relationships independently. Responds promptly and
    professionally. Handles routine queries without escalation.
  → To reach Advanced: Own the relationship for one client. Proactively check in between formal meetings.

  Advanced
    Builds strong, long-term client partnerships. Proactively identifies client
    concerns before they escalate. Manages complex or difficult client
    relationships.
  → To reach Expert: Take on a demanding client. Focus on understanding their real concerns beneath the surface.

  Expert
    Trusted strategic partner to senior client stakeholders. Influences client
    decisions. Turns difficult relationships into strong ones. Recognised as a
    go-to by clients.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Escalation Handling
│ Managing client complaints, urgent issues, and escalations — staying calm
│ under pressure, taking ownership, and driving resolution while keeping
│ stakeholders informed.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Recognises when an issue needs escalating. Can communicate basic status
    updates to clients.
  → To reach Proficient: Observe how a senior manager handles a live escalation. Note the communication approach.

  Proficient
    Handles standard escalations independently. Takes ownership, coordinates
    internally, keeps client informed throughout.
  → To reach Advanced: Take ownership of your next escalation from start to finish. Write up a lessons-learned note afterwards.

  Advanced
    Manages high-pressure escalations across multiple schemes. De-escalates tense
    situations. Identifies root causes and prevents recurrence.
  → To reach Expert: Analyse a pattern of escalations across schemes. Propose a structural fix.

  Expert
    Handles critical incidents at a senior level. Defines the escalation
    framework. Coaches others on escalation management.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Service Reviews & Reporting
│ Preparing and running regular client service reviews — presenting MI,
│ discussing performance against SLAs, and agreeing actions.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Contributes to service review preparation. Can present simple metrics with
    guidance.
  → To reach Proficient: Help prepare the MI pack for an upcoming review. Understand what each metric means.

  Proficient
    Prepares and runs standard service reviews. Presents MI clearly. Agrees
    actions and follows up.
  → To reach Advanced: Present a section of a service review. Get feedback from the client and your manager.

  Advanced
    Runs strategic service reviews. Interprets trends in MI. Drives continuous
    improvement conversations with clients.
  → To reach Expert: Lead a full review end-to-end. Prepare a narrative that tells a story with the data.

  Expert
    Defines the service review framework. Uses reviews to deepen client
    relationships and spot commercial opportunities.

CATEGORY: Service Delivery
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ SLA & Performance Management
│ Monitoring, reporting, and managing performance against Service Level
│ Agreements — understanding what's at risk, taking corrective action, and
│ driving consistent delivery.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Understands what SLAs are and why they matter. Can check status of items
    against deadlines with guidance.
  → To reach Proficient: Learn what SLAs apply to your schemes. Understand the consequences of breach.

  Proficient
    Monitors SLA performance across schemes independently. Identifies items at
    risk. Escalates appropriately when thresholds are breached.
  → To reach Advanced: Track SLA status weekly. Build a habit of flagging risks early rather than reporting failures.

  Advanced
    Manages SLA performance proactively. Analyses breach patterns. Works with
    team leads to address root causes. Reports confidently to clients.
  → To reach Expert: Analyse breach trends over a quarter. Present findings and a proposed action plan.

  Expert
    Defines and negotiates SLAs. Designs monitoring and reporting frameworks.
    Drives systemic improvements to hit targets consistently.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Incident & Problem Management
│ Responding to service incidents — coordinating resolution, communicating with
│ stakeholders, and conducting post-incident reviews to prevent recurrence.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Aware of the incident process. Can log and track incidents with guidance.
  → To reach Proficient: Read through a previous major incident report. Understand the timeline and what was learned.

  Proficient
    Manages incidents independently. Coordinates resolution across teams.
    Communicates status clearly to stakeholders.
  → To reach Advanced: Lead the next incident that comes your way from detection to resolution.

  Advanced
    Handles major incidents across multiple schemes. Runs post-incident reviews.
    Identifies systemic issues and drives fixes.
  → To reach Expert: Run a post-incident review for a significant issue. Produce an action plan and track it to completion.

  Expert
    Defines incident management processes. Builds resilience into service
    delivery. Measures and reduces incident frequency over time.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Project & Programme Oversight
│ Overseeing delivery of calc implementation projects — tracking progress,
│ managing risks, and coordinating across coders, analysts, and the admin team.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Tracks tasks and progress on straightforward projects with guidance. Attends
    project meetings.
  → To reach Proficient: Take on ownership of tracking a project's actions log. Chase items and report status.

  Proficient
    Manages own projects to plan. Identifies risks and escalates. Runs effective
    status meetings.
  → To reach Advanced: Lead a small project end-to-end. Build a simple risk register and review it weekly.

  Advanced
    Oversees complex multi-scheme programmes. Manages dependencies across
    workstreams. Owns project governance.
  → To reach Expert: Manage a programme with multiple parallel workstreams. Use formal governance to keep it on track.

  Expert
    Runs major delivery programmes with high visibility. Defines project
    management standards. Mentors others on delivery.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Resource Planning & Capacity
│ Planning and managing team capacity — matching workload to resource,
│ anticipating pinch points, and making the case for additional resource when
│ needed.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Understands the team's current workload at a high level. Can identify when
    someone is overloaded.
  → To reach Proficient: Map out your current team's workload for the next four weeks. Spot the pinch points.

  Proficient
    Plans resource for own schemes. Balances competing priorities. Communicates
    capacity constraints clearly.
  → To reach Advanced: Build a simple capacity tracker. Use it to have an evidence-based conversation about priorities.

  Advanced
    Manages capacity across the full team. Produces forward-looking resource
    plans. Negotiates priorities with clients and project managers.
  → To reach Expert: Produce a quarterly resource plan. Share it with stakeholders and get buy-in.

  Expert
    Designs the resource planning model. Makes strategic headcount decisions.
    Uses data to drive capacity conversations with leadership.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Performance Management
│ Setting clear expectations, providing regular feedback, conducting appraisals,
│ and managing performance issues — helping people succeed and addressing
│ problems early.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Understands the performance management process. Participates in appraisal
    conversations.
  → To reach Proficient: Ensure your 1:1s have a consistent structure. Prepare in advance and follow up on actions.

  Proficient
    Conducts regular 1:1s. Sets objectives and gives feedback. Manages standard
    performance situations.
  → To reach Advanced: Write SMART objectives with each of your team. Review them quarterly.

  Advanced
    Handles complex performance issues constructively. Builds development plans.
    Coaches people to improve sustainably.
  → To reach Expert: Handle a performance concern early with a direct conversation. Seek HR guidance where needed.

  Expert
    Defines performance frameworks. Creates a high-performance culture. Coaches
    other managers on performance conversations.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Development & Coaching
│ Identifying development needs, creating growth opportunities, and coaching
│ team members — helping people build skills and progress their careers.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Discusses development with team members. Aware of available training
    resources.
  → To reach Proficient: Ask each team member what they want to develop. Connect it to a concrete opportunity.

  Proficient
    Creates individual development plans. Identifies opportunities (training,
    stretch tasks, mentoring). Gives constructive feedback.
  → To reach Advanced: Build a development plan for each direct report. Review it in 1:1s quarterly.

  Advanced
    Coaches team members effectively. Connects development to career goals.
    Builds a learning culture within the team.
  → To reach Expert: Use coaching questions instead of giving answers. Help people solve their own problems.

  Expert
    Designs team development programmes. Spots future talent. Builds pipelines
    for succession. Influences L&D strategy.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Team Engagement & Culture
│ Building a motivated, cohesive team — creating psychological safety,
│ recognising good work, managing wellbeing, and fostering a positive working
│ environment.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Aware of team morale and basic wellbeing needs. Creates a respectful
    environment.
  → To reach Proficient: Say thank you specifically and promptly when someone does good work. Be consistent.

  Proficient
    Actively recognises good work. Checks in on wellbeing. Builds a team
    environment where people feel comfortable raising concerns.
  → To reach Advanced: Run a retrospective on how the team is working together. Act on the feedback.

  Advanced
    Drives team engagement initiatives. Spots and addresses morale issues early.
    Creates a culture of trust, feedback, and continuous improvement.
  → To reach Expert: Measure team engagement (pulse surveys, 1:1 conversation). Use the data to take action.

  Expert
    Shapes the team's culture deliberately. Builds high-trust environments.
    Recognised as a manager people want to work for.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Conflict & Difficult Conversations
│ Addressing conflict between team members, managing difficult conversations
│ with direct reports or stakeholders, and maintaining professional
│ relationships under pressure.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Recognises conflict. Can have straightforward corrective conversations with
    guidance.
  → To reach Proficient: Don't avoid difficult conversations. Prepare what you want to say and have them promptly.

  Proficient
    Addresses conflict directly and constructively. Has difficult 1:1
    conversations calmly and professionally.
  → To reach Advanced: Use a structure for difficult conversations (e.g. SBI: Situation-Behaviour-Impact).

  Advanced
    Mediates between team members. Handles sensitive HR situations. Maintains
    relationships through challenging conversations.
  → To reach Expert: Mediate a conflict between two team members. Focus on understanding both perspectives.

  Expert
    Coaches others on conflict resolution. Creates a culture where issues are
    raised early, not left to fester.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Scheme & Legislation Awareness
│ Sufficient understanding of pension scheme rules, key legislation, and
│ regulatory requirements to have credible client conversations, oversee
│ delivery, and spot when something doesn't look right.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Aware of the main pension calculation types. Can follow a high-level
    conversation about scheme benefits.
  → To reach Proficient: Read a scheme booklet end-to-end for one of your schemes. Ask an analyst to explain the key calcs.

  Proficient
    Understands the key benefit structures (DB, DC, hybrid). Familiar with major
    legislation (GMP, McCloud, dashboards). Can discuss schemes confidently with
    clients.
  → To reach Advanced: Follow industry news (Pensions Age, TPR updates). Understand what's coming and what it means for your schemes.

  Advanced
    Strong breadth of knowledge across scheme types and legislation. Spots
    regulatory risks. Can interpret scheme rules well enough to challenge
    technical output.
  → To reach Expert: Study a recent legislative change in detail (e.g. McCloud remedy). Understand the operational implications.

  Expert
    Deep overview knowledge across all schemes managed. Consulted on regulatory
    impact. Keeps team updated on legislative changes and their operational
    implications.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Calculation Awareness
│ Understanding enough about how pension calculations work to manage the
│ delivery team, sense-check outputs, and explain the process to clients —
│ without needing to spec or code calcs yourself.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Knows the main calc types exist (quotes, retirements, deaths, transfers). Can
    describe the high-level process.
  → To reach Proficient: Ask a coder or analyst to walk you through a completed calc from spec to output.

  Proficient
    Understands the end-to-end calc delivery process. Can follow a worked example
    at a high level. Knows what coders and analysts each do.
  → To reach Advanced: Sit in on a UAT session. Understand what the admin team are testing and why.

  Advanced
    Understands where complexity arises in calcs (GMP, tax, edge cases). Can have
    a technical conversation with coders and analysts. Challenges timelines and
    quality credibly.
  → To reach Expert: Read a calculation spec for a scheme you manage. Identify the parts you don't understand and ask.

  Expert
    Understands calc delivery deeply enough to set standards, spot systemic
    issues, and represent the team's work to the most technical client contacts.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Regulatory & Compliance Awareness
│ Understanding the regulatory environment — TPR expectations, HMRC
│ requirements, GDPR, audit requirements — and ensuring the service operates
│ within compliance boundaries.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Aware that regulation governs pension administration. Follows compliance
    procedures set by others.
  → To reach Proficient: Read your organisation's data protection and pension compliance policies.

  Proficient
    Understands key regulatory requirements for pension services. Ensures team
    follows compliance procedures. Flags potential issues.
  → To reach Advanced: Follow TPR and HMRC updates. Understand which ones affect your service.

  Advanced
    Monitors regulatory developments. Assesses operational impact. Ensures the
    team is ahead of compliance requirements.
  → To reach Expert: Lead a compliance review for your schemes. Identify any gaps and build an action plan.

  Expert
    Defines the compliance framework for the service. Owns regulatory risk.
    Represents the service in audits and regulatory reviews.

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Communication & Influencing
│ Communicating effectively at all levels — with clients, senior leadership,
│ technical teams, and your own team — in writing and verbally, and influencing
│ outcomes through clear thinking and credible delivery.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Communicates clearly within the team and with clients on day-to-day matters.
  → To reach Proficient: Practice summarising a complex situation in three clear sentences. Get feedback.

  Proficient
    Communicates confidently across stakeholder groups. Writes clear reports and
    emails. Presents in meetings with credibility.
  → To reach Advanced: Volunteer to present at a client meeting or internal leadership update.

  Advanced
    Handles complex or sensitive communications. Adapts style to audience.
    Influences decisions at a senior level.
  → To reach Expert: Work on your written communication. Get a trusted colleague to review important emails or reports.

  Expert
    Exceptional communicator. Represents the service at the highest levels.
    Shapes narratives that land with boards and executive stakeholders.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Problem Solving & Decision Making
│ Analysing problems, making sound decisions under uncertainty, and driving
│ solutions — balancing speed with thoroughness.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Solves straightforward operational problems. Escalates when uncertain.
  → To reach Proficient: When facing a decision, write down the options and their trade-offs before choosing.

  Proficient
    Makes good decisions independently on standard issues. Analyses situations
    before acting. Communicates reasoning clearly.
  → To reach Advanced: Review a decision you made recently. What would you do differently? What did you learn?

  Advanced
    Tackles complex, ambiguous problems. Makes decisions with incomplete
    information. Thinks through second-order effects.
  → To reach Expert: Take on a problem that spans multiple teams. Coordinate the solution end-to-end.

  Expert
    Solves systemic, cross-functional problems. Anticipates issues before they
    arise. Known for sound judgement in difficult situations.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Continuous Improvement
│ Identifying opportunities to improve processes, quality, and efficiency in
│ the service — and driving those improvements through to implementation.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Notices inefficiencies and suggests ideas. Participates in improvement
    initiatives.
  → To reach Proficient: Keep a list of friction points you notice each week. Pick one to fix each month.

  Proficient
    Identifies and implements improvements within own area. Uses evidence to
    prioritise. Tracks outcomes.
  → To reach Advanced: Propose a specific process improvement with a clear rationale and success measure.

  Advanced
    Drives improvement programmes across the service. Engages stakeholders.
    Delivers measurable results.
  → To reach Expert: Lead a service improvement project. Measure the before-and-after impact.

  Expert
    Builds a continuous improvement culture. Uses structured methodologies.
    Transforms service performance over time.


┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
│ Time Management & Prioritisation
│ Managing a complex workload — balancing strategic priorities with day-to-day
│ operational demands, and making clear decisions about where to focus.
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Foundation
    Completes tasks on time with priorities set by others. Manages own diary.
  → To reach Proficient: Block time for your most important priorities. Treat that time as sacrosanct.

  Proficient
    Manages competing demands effectively. Communicates proactively when
    overloaded. Keeps stakeholders informed.
  → To reach Advanced: Audit how you spend your time for a week. Identify what to stop, delegate, or reduce.

  Advanced
    Prioritises strategically across multiple schemes and workstreams. Delegates
    effectively. Protects time for high-value work.
  → To reach Expert: Practise saying no to low-value requests with a clear reason.

  Expert
    Optimises team workflow as well as personal productivity. Removes systemic
    bottlenecks. Balances short-term delivery with long-term investment.
