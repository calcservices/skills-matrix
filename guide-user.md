# Skills Self-Assessment — User Guide

A quick self-assessment that captures your current skill levels. It takes around 10 minutes, your progress saves automatically, and you only need to do it once — though you can update it at any time.

---

## Steps

### 1. Open the file
Open `index.html` in your browser. No internet connection or installation needed.

### 2. Select your profile
Use the toggle in the top-right corner to select your role: **Coders**, **Analysts**, or **Service Managers**. This loads the skill set relevant to you.

### 3. Enter your name
Type your full name in the box at the top. This is used to identify your export file.

### 4. Rate yourself for each skill
Work through each category and click the level that best describes you. There are four levels:

| Level | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Awareness | You know the concept and have some exposure |
| 2 | Practitioner | You use this skill regularly with confidence |
| 3 | Advanced | You apply it in complex situations and can guide others |
| 4 | Expert | You are a go-to authority and drive best practice |

Click **"What does this mean?"** next to any skill to see a full description of what each level looks like in practice.

### 5. Check your progress
The progress bar near the top shows how many skills you've rated. Completed categories collapse automatically to keep things tidy. The radar chart gives you a visual overview of your profile.

### 6. Save & Export when done
Click **Save & Export** in the top-right corner. This downloads a JSON file named with your name and today's date — for example:

```
skills-jane-smith-2026-03-24-143215.json
```

Send this file to your line manager.

### 7. Coming back later
Your ratings are saved automatically in your browser. Open the file again at any time to update your assessment. Each time you export, a new snapshot is added to your history.

Click the **My History** tab to see all your past exports side by side.

---

> **Tip:** Be honest — there are no wrong answers. The goal is to give your manager a clear picture of where you are now, so they can support your development. If you're unsure between two levels, pick the lower one and add a note when you send the file.
